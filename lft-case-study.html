What Makes "Looking for Trouble" a Really Useful Thing for Charities?

A "Really Useful Thing" (RUT) is a single-purpose tool for a persistent issue. For small charities, that issue is firefighting: a reactive state where urgent crises prevent long-term "fireproofing." Looking for Trouble is an operational smoke detector designed to turn these crises into resilient data.

Dual-Action Scenarios

Every simulator scenario moves the user from a quick fix to a permanent cure:

  • Firefighting Action (Quick Fix): A pragmatic, 5-minute step to "stop the bleeding" (e.g., notifying auditors immediately about missing receipts).
  • The Opportunity (Long-Term Fix): A policy or behavioral change to prevent recurrence (e.g., transitioning to 100% digital claims).

Frictionless Implementation

Instead of generating PDFs that get buried in inboxes, the simulator exports action plans directly to WhatsApp. This ensures immediate visibility for frontline staff and allows for easy customization before sharing.

AI-Driven Evolution

By feeding WhatsApp chat logs back into an AI assistant, charities can detect recurring trends. This creates a continuous feedback loop: the AI identifies emerging operational bottlenecks and generates new, hyper-realistic scenarios based on the charity's actual, evolving realities.
